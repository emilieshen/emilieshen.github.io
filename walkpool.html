walkpool

Individually designed and prototyped a mobile app that increases safety for walking. This project spanned 15 weeks.

Tools Used: Sketch, Invision Studio, Adobe Premiere Pro, Adobe Illustrator, Keynote

Research

Approximately 4 in 10 Americans do not feel safe walking alone in their own neighborhood. Many crimes happen to people who are walking by themself--robbery, assault, rape, etc. I interviewed several individuals to determine what factors of walking make them feel unsafe, why, and what they thought would help make them feel more at ease. Upon research, I aggregated my findings and data to develop three possible personas/users.

How might we effectively educate visitors in an interactive and meaningful way?



Three personas were also created as a result of our interviews and survey conducted: frequent visitors, occassional visitors, and students.


Frequent Visitor

Name: Catherine
Age: 34
Description: Catherine lives in New York and works as a financial analyst. She loves going to museums. Catherine tries to visit a museum at least once a month, and she has gone to certain museums more than once, but she also likes to explore many new museums.
Goals: Experience better navigation at museums; wants to learn about new exhibits at museums

Occassional Visitor

Name: Amanda
Age: 30
Description: Amanda works in New York as a lawyer. She used to go to museums a lot when she was a student, but now she is too busy to visit museums. She gets off work in the evening and most museums close at that time.
Goals: Visit museums in New York during free time

Student

Name: Tom
Age: 19
Description: Tom doesn’t have much spare time to visit museums. He usually only goes to museums when he has to do homework that requires him to visit a museum. He will also visit museums if a friend is visiting and would like to go.
Goals: Find something specific in the museum for homework; find a place to hang out with his friends


Competitive Analysis

I conducted a competitive analysis, looking at what solutions are currently out there to increase safety while walking, and looking at how these solutions could be improved.
